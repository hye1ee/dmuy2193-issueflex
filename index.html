The Ethical Side of Neurotechnology
For The Live Like a Human Being
The Hidden Side
In recent years, the field of neurotechnology has witnessed unprecedented advancements, with companies like Neuralink achieving remarkable milestones, such as successfully implanting electric chips into the human brain. As we explore the potential benefits of such innovations, particularly in enhancing the quality of life for individuals with disabilities, it becomes crucial to delve into the ethical considerations and potential side effects. While neurotechnology holds immense promise for medical breakthroughs and improved human capabilities, it also raises ethical dilemmas surrounding privacy, consent, and the potential misuse of neuroenhancements.
